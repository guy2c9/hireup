



















Hireup – Project Evolution 2cloudnine Process Changes
Prepared by Julian McGrath, December 2025
Purpose
The purpose of this document is to outline the end-to-end process design supporting shift-level payroll tax determination, For Profit (FP) and Not For Profit (NFP) classification, and associated GL processing required for Hireup’s transition under Project Evolution.
This document explains how payroll tax is determined at a shift level, how FP and NFP work is differentiated, how state-based payroll tax rules are applied, and how payroll tax impacts are reflected through journals and retrospective adjustments.
Requirement
Under Project Evolution, Hireup’s operating and legal entity model requires payroll tax to be calculated based on the actual nature and location of work performed, rather than assignment-level defaults.
Hireup’s requirements include:
	•	Determining payroll tax obligations at a shift level
	•	Supporting workers who perform work across multiple states
	•	Supporting mixed FP and NFP work within the same assignment and pay period
	•	Applying payroll tax to FP shifts regardless of assignment defaults
	•	Supporting State Revenue Office (SRO) rulings that may require payroll tax to apply to all shifts in a month
	•	Reflecting correct payroll tax outcomes through GL journals, including reversals and corrections
Assignment-level payroll tax determination alone is insufficient to meet these requirements.
Solution Overview
The solution introduces a shift-level payroll tax processing model that integrates payroll calculation and GL processing.
The design is based on three core principles:
	•	Shift-level determination of work location and FP/NFP classification
	•	State-based employment cost items configured separately for FP and NFP work
	•	Controlled month-end recalculation and journal correction processes
Together, these elements ensure payroll tax is calculated accurately, reported correctly, and remains auditable throughout Hireup’s Project Evolution transition.

Solution Design
The solution consists of five coordinated stages:
	•	Shift-Level Location Determination
	•	FP / NFP Shift Classification
	•	Payroll Tax Calculation Using State-Based Employment Cost Items
	•	Month-End Reclassification & Payroll Tax Recalculation
	•	GL Journal Generation, Reversal, and Correction
Each stage is described below.
Stage 1 – Shift-Level Location Determination
Purpose
Stage 1 ensures payroll tax rules are applied based on the actual state in which work was performed.
Processing Logic
For each shift:
	•	If a location is supplied at the timesheet (shift) level, that location is used to determine state-based payroll tax rules
	•	If no shift-level location is supplied, the system falls back to the assignment’s location
This maintains backward compatibility while enabling accurate cross-border payroll tax determination.
Stage 2 – FP / NFP Shift Classification
Purpose
Stage 2 determines whether each shift is classified as For Profit (FP) or Not For Profit (NFP) for payroll tax purposes.
Processing Logic
For each shift:
	•	FP/NFP classification is derived using Hireup-defined rules and indicators
	•	FP shifts are flagged as requiring payroll tax consideration
	•	NFP shifts are excluded from payroll tax unless overridden by month-end rules
This supports mixed FP/NFP workloads and occasional FP work within otherwise NFP assignments.
Stage 3 – Payroll Tax Calculation Using Employment Cost Items
Purpose
Stage 3 applies payroll tax using state-based employment cost items that differentiate FP and NFP work.
Processing Logic
For each state:
	•	Two employment cost items exist:
	•	FP cost item – payroll tax applied at the legislated state rate
	•	NFP cost item – payroll tax rate set to 0%
When payroll tax is calculated:
	•	The system selects the appropriate employment cost item based on:
	•	The shift’s determined state
	•	The shift’s FP/NFP classification
This ensures payroll tax is applied only where required and flows consistently into payroll and GL outputs.
Stage 4 – Month-End Reclassification & Payroll Tax Recalculation
Purpose
Stage 4 supports Hireup’s requirement to reassess payroll tax obligations at month-end, including retrospective recalculation across earlier pay periods in the month.
Processing Logic
At the final pay run of the month:
	•	The system determines which payroll tax calculation mode applies:
	•	FP-only (payroll tax applies only to FP shifts), or
	•	Part/All method (payroll tax applies to all shifts, where required by SRO ruling)
Where recalculation is required:
	•	All shifts in the month are re-evaluated
	•	Original payroll tax amounts are compared to recalculated values
	•	Any differences are identified for adjustment
This ensures Hireup’s payroll tax liability aligns with regulatory requirements.
Stage 5 – GL Journal Generation, Reversal & Correction
Purpose
Stage 5 ensures that payroll tax outcomes are accurately reflected in Hireup’s general ledger.
Processing Logic
When payroll tax recalculation identifies differences:
	•	Reversal journals are generated to negate previously posted payroll tax amounts
	•	Correction journals are generated to post updated payroll tax values
	•	Journals clearly distinguish:
	•	Original postings
	•	Reversals
	•	Corrections
GL outputs reflect FP/NFP classification, state-based treatment, and the calculation mode applied, ensuring clear auditability.
Reporting and Auditability
The solution ensures end-to-end auditability by:
	•	Retaining FP/NFP classification at a shift level
	•	Recording payroll tax calculation mode at month-end
	•	Linking reversal and correction journals to original postings
	•	Supporting detailed reporting for reconciliation and compliance review
End-to-End Outcome
At the completion of the shift-level payroll tax and GL processing flow:
	•	Payroll tax is calculated accurately per shift
	•	Cross-border and mixed FP/NFP work is fully supported
	•	Month-end payroll tax obligations are correctly reassessed
	•	GL journals accurately reflect payroll tax liabilities
	•	Hireup’s Project Evolution requirements are met in a controlled, auditable manner
